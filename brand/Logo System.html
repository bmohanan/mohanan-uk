The ~bmc mark.

A terminal-style monogram on the gradient that defines the brand. The tilde nods to home directories and command-line fluency — the consultancy that ships from a prompt, not a slide.

// 01 — Primary mark

brand/logo/primary
~bmc
~bmc

// anatomy

  • shapeRounded square. Radius scales linearly with the side — 12px @ 52, 38px @ 168.
  • fillLinear gradient 135° · #6a3df5 → #d136e6 @ 55% → #3a7df5.
  • glazeRadial highlight from top-left, ~22% white, fades by 55%.
  • typeJetBrains Mono 500, lowercase, letterspacing −.04em.
  • tildeAlways rendered at 72% opacity, separated by .08em.

// behaviour

  • on darkAdd the glow shadow — magenta-purple, 8px down, blurred 28px.
  • on paperDrop the glow. Gradient is enough; no flat-fill replacement needed.
  • smallBelow 24px, drop to the favicon / single-character fallback.
  • printGradient prints clean on coated stock. For uncoated, use the monochrome ink variant.

// 05 — Clear space & minimums

brand/logo/space
~bmc
↑ ½× height ↓ ½× height ← →

Reserve at least ½ the badge height of clear space on every side. Never place type, rules, or other marks inside this zone. Minimum legible size: 24px on screen, 8mm in print.

// 07 — Do & don't

brand/logo/rules
DO
~bmc
Use the badge at its native aspect ratio with the gradient and glow on dark surfaces.
DON'T
~bmc
Don't skew, rotate, or stretch the mark. Lay it flat or don't lay it.
DON'T
~bmc
Don't recolour the gradient. The three stops are the brand.
DO
~bmc
Use the outline variants when you can't reproduce the gradient — single-colour print, stamp, embroidery.
DON'T
~bmc
No drop-shadows, no offset duplicates, no Photoshop effects. The glow is the only shadow.
DON'T
~bmc
Don't change the shape. The rounded square is part of the mark; circles are for avatars only.
